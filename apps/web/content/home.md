The **Marine Mammal Assessment Platform (MMAP)** helps researchers collect standardized health assessments in the field—even without connectivity—and contribute to a curated open dataset.

## Why MMAP exists

Marine mammal health assessments require coordinated boats, specialized handlers, and rapid data capture under harsh conditions. Spreadsheets and ad hoc forms make sync, validation, and public release slow. MMAP provides:

- **Offline-first field capture** on tablets with schema validation
- **Reliable sync** when marina Wi‑Fi returns
- **Open schemas** so new species protocols can be added without rewriting the stack
- **Public dataset exports** under an open license

## Belize manatee assessment context

The first protocol—`manatee_v1`—supports the annual Belize manatee assessment led in collaboration with **Jamal Galves** and the **Clearwater Marine Aquarium Research Institute (CMARI)**. Field teams coordinate multiple boats, drone localization, onboard veterinary assessment, and release.

**Jacob Tindall** and CMARI advisors help ensure the workflow reflects real capture safety, measurement order, and naming conventions used in Belize operations.
